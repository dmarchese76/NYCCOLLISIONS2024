Why Congestion Pricing is necessary in NYC?

Congestion pricing gets the green light set to take effect Jan. 5th

Delfina Marchese

Nov.21, 2024

The MTA Board has approved the revised congestion pricing plan, a project that will charge a toll to vehicles entering Manhattan at or below 60 street-near Central Park. Gov. Kathy Hochul revived the plan shortly after Trump was elected President.

The toll aims to fund public transit improvements, cut air pollution, and help reduce fatalities and persons injured in traffic collisions.

According to Transportation Alternatives, an advocacy group focused on traffic safety throughout New York City, 193 people were killed and 2,338 seriously injured in traffic collisions throughout the city in the first nine months of 2024.

“This year, more people are killed by cars and trucks than are killed by guns in New York City,” said Alexa Sledge, communications director of Transportation Alternatives.”It's a very, very serious public health crisis, and we're just not taking it seriously enough.”
